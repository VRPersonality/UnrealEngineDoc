4/**
5 * PackageMap implementation that is client/connection specific. This subclass implements all NetGUID Acking and interactions with a UConnection.
6 * On the server, each client will have their own instance of UPackageMapClient.
7 *
8 * UObjects are first serialized as <NetGUID, Name/Path> pairs. UPackageMapClient tracks each NetGUID's usage and knows when a NetGUID has been ACKd.
9 * Once ACK'd, objects are just serialized as <NetGUID>. The result is higher bandwidth usage upfront for new clients, but minimal bandwidth once
10 * things gets going.
11 *
12 * A further optimization is enabled by default. References will actually be serialized via:
13 * <NetGUID, <(Outer *), Object Name> >. Where Outer * is a reference to the UObject's Outer.
14 *
15 * The main advantages from this are:
16 * -Flexibility. No precomputed net indices or large package lists need to be exchanged for UObject serialization.
17 * -Cross version communication. The name is all that is needed to exchange references.
18 * -Efficiency in that a very small % of UObjects will ever be serialized. Only Objects that serialized are assigned NetGUIDs.
19 */
