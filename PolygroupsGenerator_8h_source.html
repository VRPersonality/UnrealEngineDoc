112 /**
113 * Incrementally compute approximate-geodesic-based furthest-point sampling of the mesh until NumPoints
114 * samples have been found, then compute local geodesic patches (eg approximate surface voronoi diagaram).
115 * Optionally weight geodesic-distance computation, which will produce different patch shapes.
116 * If StartingGroups are provided, then new groups are constrained to subdivide existing groups, ie
117 * none of the new groups will cross the boundaries of the StartingGroups
118 */

123 FPolygroupSet* StartingGroups = nullptr);

125 /**
126 *
127 * Similar to the technique used in FindPolygroupsFromFurthestPointSampling, except this algorithm
128 * attempts to select sample points in connected mesh regions based on the rough area of each region,
129 * granting more initial points to larger regions and fewer points to smaller regions, minium of one.
130 * This approach handles cases better when there is a large varience in region sizes and a large number
131 * of small regions compared to large regions. In this case, the alternative routine can over focus on
132 * small regions, granting the larger regions too few points. This leads to a relative over sampling of
133 * small regions and creates large polygroups out of the larger regions instead of more equitably breaking
134 * up larger regions into multiple groups.
135 *
136 * In this algorithm, NumPoints is less a strict target of final patches, but a rough guideline to direct
137 * the area sampler on how much total area each sample should occupy. E.g. a value of 100 would indicate
138 * we ideally want 100 equal sized regions of the mesh. However, since each connected region must have at
139 * least one sample, the final count may not be exactly NumPoints. Instead, each region, and it's area, is
140 * considered independently according to this desired "sample density". A "perfect" mesh (with one connected
141 * region and a high tesselation of equal sized triangles) should generate equal sized patches of NumPoints
142 * in count. A poorer quality mesh will result in a best effort take at this, while ensuring all triangles
143 * are part of a group.
144 *
145 */
146 UE_API bool FindPolygroupsFromAreaDensityPointSampling(
147 int32 NumPoints,
148 EWeightingType WeightingType,
149 FVector3d WeightingCoeffs = FVector3d::One(),
150 FPolygroupSet* StartingGroups = nullptr);
